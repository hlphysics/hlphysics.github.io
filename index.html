S.U.V.A.T. equations

The SUVAT equations are the fundemantal equations for kinematics. They are:

  • $s=ut$
  • $v=u+at$
  • $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$
  • $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

In these equations these are the following variables:

  • s = distance ($m$)
  • u = initial speed ($ms^{-1}$)
  • v = final speed ($ms{-1}$)
  • a = acceleration ($ms{-2}$)
  • t = time ($s$)

Newton's Laws of Motion

Isaac Newton observed the motion of objects and concluded that:

  • 1st Law: An object remains stationary or moving at uniform speed unless an external force is applied.
  • 2nd Law: Force is the rate of change of Momentum($P$). $F=\frac{\DeltaP}{\Deltat}=\frac{mv-mu}{\Deltat}=m\frac{v-u}{t}=ma$
  • 3rd Law: If object A exerts a force on object B, object B will exert an equal and opposite force on object A.

Projectiles

Projectiles are objects shot at an angle $\theta$ (theta), with a certain velocity. Normally projectile questions are based around calculating where and when it hits the ground, with what angle and with which force.

These questions can be broken up into these two equations:

  • $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ for the vertical motion
  • $s = ut$ for the horizontal motion

The original velocity can be divided into vertical and horizontal speeds, with $u\sin\theta$ for vertical speed and $u\cos\theta$ for horizontal speed.

Energy

We initially study the following concepts:

  • Kinetic Energy=$E_{k}$: Energy associated with motion. $E_{k}= \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
  • Potential(gravitational) Energy=$E_{p}$: Energy associated with height. $E_{p}=mgh$ $g$ = gravity
  • Work Done=$WD$: Movement in the direction of the force. $WD=Fs$ (force $x$ distance travelled in the direction of the force)

All energy is measured in Joules(J)

Momentum

Momentum is the quantity of movement in a body. It's a vector that can be obtained through $P = mv$ or $P = \root2{2mE_{k}}$

Momentum gives to the Laws of Conservation of Momentum:

  • Elastic collision: Momentum is conserved. $E_{k}$ is conserved
  • Inelastic collision: Momentum is conserved. $E_{k}$ is NOT conserved

NOTE: Momentum is always conserved in a closed system. $P_{initial}=P_{final}$
