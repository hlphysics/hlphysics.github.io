Relativity

Relativity is the study of the motion of light, and objects travelling at a fraction of the speed of light.

Physics tells us that when two observers are viewing an object, they will see that object in different places relative to their reference point. This is also true if an object is in motion. If Observer 1 is stationary and Observer 2 is moving at a constant velocity of $5ms^{-1}$, an object moving at a velocity of $-5ms^{-1}$ will go at different speeds for each observer. Observer 1, whose frame of reference is still, will think the object is going at $-5ms^{-1}$, for Observer 2 (whose frame of reference is in motion) however, every second Observer 2 moves 5 metres to one side, the object will move 5 metres to the other side, making it seem to Observer 2 that the object is moving at a speed of $-10ms^{-1}$
